Whats trending, GBV news headlines

Since the onset of the lockdown in Zimbabwe on 30 March, gender based violence cases have increased by 70% with 2, 768 cases recorded by only Musasa Project within the period from 30 March to 27 June. If all these cases can be reported by one project, what about those reported to the police and those which were not reported?...Read more

Despite how hard the women in our communities work, how can they now survive these lockdown restrictions? According to a report, 60% of the people in Zimbabwe rely on the informal sector industry which is currently not running due to the pandemic....Read more here! We will thus want to ensure that the affected women in Goodhope earn a living without necessary traveling to the city centers.

Not only is the GBV affecting the women, it is also affecting the children. How can they even concentrate at school knowing that at home its always fights after fights. To safeguard the children's future, we thus maked sure that we birth this initiative. According to UNICEF, during the pandemic, children have been at heightened risk of abuse...Read more

Surely we can not smile at that!!!. #stopGBV#womenempowerement.
